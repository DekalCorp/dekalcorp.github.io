Iterating on mechanical systems with conventional CAD is inefficient. Models take minutes to rebuild and break with small design changes.

Dekal solves this with implicit modeling, generating robust parametric geometry in milliseconds.

How does it work?

Conventional "BRep" CAD

  • Object is defined by a collection of 2D surfaces mapped to 3D space
  • Requires slow sequential execution
  • Breaks when topology changes

Implicit CAD

  • Object is defined through functions that fold the 4th dimension onto 3D space
  • Robust against any topology change
  • Fast by being massively parallelizable on the GPU

FAQ

  • How does modeling using implicits look like?

    Similar to conventional CAD, implicit models are generated through a series of operations that produce the geometry. Those operations can be defined through code or through a visual interface. However, each new step can only refer to a previous step and can't refer to a feature of the generated model itself. You can't select a specific edge to be chamfered for example. At least not yet...

  • What are the design areas where implicit modeling shines?

    There are multiples areas where implicit modeling shines, here are some examples: - Early in the product development phase when you want to rapidely iterate over potential design implementations and evalutate their performance - For complex structures that are manufactured through aditive manufacturing - For the generation of manufactruing tooling

  • What are the domains where implicit modeking is less effective than BRep CAD?

    Implicit modeling is typically less effective during the detailed design process. For example, implementing specific changes during a design for manufacturing process, is difficult in implicit modeling since you can't select specific features on the model itself that should be modified.

  • What user interface does Dekal provide?

    Dekal offers a conventional visual design workbench as well as a scripting interface to generate model through code.

  • Can I generate models using AI / LLMs?

    Yes, as opposed to conventional BRep CAD, implicit modeling is particularly well suited to be generated through AI since the model is entriely described through the series of step that generated it and the topology doesn't depend on the kernel.

  • Why is it called "implicit"?

    Because you don't know what the model looks like until you evaluate the function that produces it. It's implicitely deifned by the function that produces it. In conventional CAD, the model is stored as a series of 2D surfaces mapped to the 3D space explicitely. If you remove the steps that created the explicit model, you could still keep and inspect the model, since it's explicitely defined.
